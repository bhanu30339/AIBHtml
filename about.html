Pilot Digital Internship Program

Digital Engagement (2020)

Initial Phase

During 2020, a series of online engagement sessions were conducted with Indian university students to support youth engagement, resilience, and cross-border collaboration.

These interactions highlighted the need for an ongoing digital platform that could connect students in India with opportunities linked to Australia–India cooperation.

Internship Implementation

Practical Experience

In response, a pilot digital internship program was implemented in collaboration with an Indian university. Postgraduate management students participated remotely, working on projects designed to complement the Victorian Government's India Strategy.

The internships provided students with practical exposure to international policy, market research, and strategic analysis while enabling sustained academic and professional engagement between Australia and India.

Key Success Factor

The pilot demonstrated the effectiveness of virtual internships as a scalable model for international collaboration and skills development. This innovative approach created new pathways for student engagement during challenging times.

COVID-19 Response & Student Support (2020–2021)

During the COVID-19 pandemic, AusInd Bridge Foundation (AIBF) delivered targeted, non-commercial programmes focused on international student welfare, mental health, and Australia–India community engagement.

International Student Welfare & Relief Support (Victoria)

  • Coordinated food and grocery assistance for Indian and other international students in Victoria who lost part-time work and income
  • Worked through community volunteers and local networks to identify students in financial distress
  • Support was confidential, need-based, and prioritised students with no income or family support

Student Mental Health & Wellbeing Support

  • Delivered online group counselling and wellbeing sessions for students located in Australia and India
  • Enabled one-to-one mentoring and emotional support for students experiencing anxiety, isolation, and uncertainty around visas and study completion
  • Sessions focused on coping strategies, mental resilience, and maintaining academic continuity

Mentoring & Career Continuity Webinars

  • Conducted structured webinars and mentoring sessions covering post-COVID study and education pathways
  • Focused on employability, career planning, health, wellbeing, and resilience during disruption
  • Speakers included academics, industry professionals, policymakers, and community leaders from Australia, India, and Southeast Asia
  • Programmes ensured continued access to guidance and learning during lockdowns

Australia–India Student & Community Engagement Dialogues

  • Facilitated digital engagement to maintain people-to-people links between Australia and India while borders were closed
  • Focused on student experiences, cross-cultural understanding, and shared recovery challenges
  • Strengthened trust between students, Australian institutions, and community leadership during the crisis
