Victoria-India Relations Enter New Era with Premier's India Strategy Launch

Deepak Vinayak OAM JP addresses historic moment at Australia India Institute

Victoria's India Strategy Launch

Australia India Institute, University of Melbourne

Historic Address

Deepak Vinayak OAM JP's keynote speech

Key Quote:

"This is a historic day when we witness Victoria's relationship with India entering a new era. Victoria's India Strategy is holistic. I want to congratulate the Hon. Daniel Andrews & his government for taking this forward."

- Deepak Vinayak OAM JP

At the prestigious Australia India Institute (University of Melbourne), Deepak Vinayak delivered a powerful address reflecting on India's diaspora and the strength of Victoria's personal connections with India during the launch of the Hon. Daniel Andrews India Strategy.

The event marked a significant milestone in bilateral relations, with the strategy representing a comprehensive framework to deepen economic, cultural, and educational ties between Victoria and India.

About Deepak Vinayak OAM JP

A respected community leader and advocate for Australia-India relations, Deepak Vinayak has been instrumental in fostering cultural and economic partnerships. His contributions were recognized with the Order of Australia Medal (OAM) and appointment as a Justice of the Peace (JP).

Strengthening Victoria–India Relations India's Independence Day Celebrations

Landmark celebrations at MCG highlight growing bilateral partnership ahead of Premier's visit to India

Historic Celebration at MCG

Uniting communities, strengthening ties

The celebration of India's Independence Day at the Melbourne Cricket Ground highlighted the enduring and growing relationship between India and Victoria. The event brought together community leaders and senior representatives of the Victorian Government, including the Deputy Premier and Minister for Education, the Hon. Ben Carroll MP, underscoring the strength of people-to-people and institutional ties.

A significant highlight was the illumination of Melbourne's iconic landmarks in the colours of the Indian tricolour, including Flinders Street Station, symbolising unity, multicultural pride and shared values. These gestures reflected Victoria's commitment to recognising the contributions of the Indian community and strengthening bilateral connections.

Premier's Upcoming Visit to India

The occasion also coincided with engagement ahead of the Hon. Jacinta Allan MP's forthcoming visit to India. A pre-departure briefing and community feedback session held at the Parliament of Victoria demonstrated the Government's inclusive approach and its focus on deepening collaboration across education, innovation, business, technology, transport, and the visitor economy.

Launch of the Parliamentarians Forum for Tech Diplomacy

Advancing Responsible, Inclusive Tech in Global Diplomacy

Global Initiative Launch

A new era in technology-driven diplomacy

We're thrilled to have launched the Parliamentarians Forum for Tech Diplomacy, a global initiative dedicated to fostering collaboration on the responsible and inclusive use of emerging technologies.

It was an honour to host Hon. Dylan Wight MP, Hon. Mathew Hilakari MP, and distinguished members of the Australian delegation from the Parliament of Victoria. This gathering, organised in partnership with the AusInd Bridge Foundation, brought together policymakers, business leaders, academics, and young changemakers from Australia and India.

Key Takeaway

A key takeaway from the event was the importance of tech-enabled diplomacy and inclusive leadership in shaping a better future. Drawing inspiration from the Hon'ble Prime Minister Narendra Modi ji's G20 model, we explored how multi-stakeholder engagement across diverse sectors can drive positive change on the global stage.

NALSAR Professor Felicitated at Parliament of Victoria

Recognition for advancing India–Australia academic ties and Campus Startup Culture across universities.

Parliament Recognition

Melbourne, Parliament of Victoria

Prof. (Dr.) Srinivas Subbarao, Dean and Professor at the Department of Management Studies and Head of the International Office at NALSAR University of Law, Hyderabad, was formally felicitated at the Parliament of Victoria for strengthening academic and people-to-people ties between India and Australia.

During his Melbourne visit, Prof. Subbarao engaged with policymakers, academic leaders, and institutions advancing collaboration under the Victoria India Strategy 2025–2030. His leadership in the Campus Startup Culture initiative—now sparking dialogue in Indian universities and gaining traction in Australia—was specially acknowledged.

The felicitation was hosted by Mr Hon.Iwan Walters, MP Parliamentary Secretary for Multicultural Affairs, at Parliament of Victoria. The visit was facilitated by AusInd Bridge Foundation, led by Prof. (Mr) Deepak Vinayak OAM JP, with support from Prof. (Mr) K K Dash, Honorary Director, India Office, AIBF.

Prof. Subbarao thanked Mr Walters for the warm reception and acknowledged the pivotal role of AIBF, Prof. Vinayak, and Prof. Dash in advancing India–Australia academic diplomacy. He also expressed gratitude to Prof. (Dr) Srikrishna Deva Rao, Vice-Chancellor of NALSAR, for visionary leadership enabling NALSAR’s global engagement.

Why it matters

Highlights growing momentum in India–Australia higher-ed collaboration and shared commitment to innovation, academic excellence, and people-to-people connections.
